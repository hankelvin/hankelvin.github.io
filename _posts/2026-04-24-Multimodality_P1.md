{% newthought "A significant amount of effort is being made across research and industry to expand on the abilities of large language models (LLMs)"%}; towards general machine ability for the <ins>understanding/perception</ins>{% sidenote "sidenote-understand" "e.g. to ingest an image and make sense of it by providing classifications of it, answering some questions about it etc..." %} and <ins>generating</ins>{% sidenote "sidenote-generate" "this is the generative element of Generative AI, e.g. to produce some new information such a text, video or speech signal." %} in other modalities (i.e. vision and speech). The public's imaginings of such capabilities were piqued by demonstrations made in the summer of 2024;{% sidenote "sidenote-imagination" "These were by OpenAI (<a href='https://openai.com/index/hello-gpt-4o/' title='Hello GPT‑4o'>GPT-4o</a>) and Kyutai AI (<a href='https://kyutai.org/blog/2024-09-18-moshi-release' title='Moshi: a speech-text foundation model for real-time dialogue'>Moshi</a>). They were rolled out against a backdrop of growing public fascination with conversational AI (e.g. ChatGPT), and fed imaginations of how we could also interact in real-time with such AI systems by taking in audio and/or visual input, receiving audio and/or visual output we can get more naturalistic interfaces with these AI models. There has also been a widening acknowledgement of the need to ground LLMs in the physical world in order to achieve more general capabilities; see <a href='https://aclanthology.org/2020.acl-main.463.pdf' title='Climbing towards NLU: On Meaning, Form, and Understanding in the Age of Data'>(Koller & Bender, 2020)</a>" %} since then, two further (and entwined) trends have emerged for these multi-/omni-modal{% sidenote "sidenote-omini" "The term 'omni' is used for models that can take input in more than one modality and generate output in more than one modality, see for instance InclusionAI's (Ant Group, Alibaba) <a href='https://huggingface.co/inclusionAI/Ming-flash-omni-2.0' title='Ming-flash-omni 2.0'>Ming-flash-omni 2.0</a> and <a href='https://huggingface.co/inclusionAI/LLaDA2.0-Uni' title='LLaDA2.0-Uni: Unifying Multimodal Understanding and Generation with Diffusion Large Language Model'>LLaDA2.0-Uni</a>."%} LLMs (MLLMs; also often appearing under the umbrella terms of "_Embodied AI_"/"_Physical AI_"), and they are reinforcing the <em>raisons d'etre</em> of such multi-/omni-modal models. 

In this post, I will briefly describe these MLLMs, try to convince you why paying at least some attention to them is useful, and then walk through how they are used in robotics and autonomous driving so as to give a more concrete sense to the terms (i.e. going beyond the buzz words that they are at the moment). I take a slight detour over an article about simulation and modeling that I really enjoyed recently, and which I found helpful to contextualise the World Models being actively pursued today. Finally, I lay out some areas I plan to write about in this space over a few more posts. _For some reason I ended up adding quite a fair bit of videos clips to this post; on mobile you might have to tap the + signs to reveal them. I hope they add to the arguments I set out to assemble in this post, if not at least you find them entertaining._

### 2️⃣ 1. What two trends?
They are: <ins>vision-language-action (VLA)</ins> models and <ins>world models (WMs)</ins>. The former (VLA) is relatively easier to define: it is essentially an LLM that can take instructions and reason (e.g. in Chain-of-Thought) together with visual input (e.g. real-time video) and instead of say just giving an answer in text as we are used to with LLMs, typically the most important output is some set of information that can be used for controlling a robot's movements.{% sidenote "sidenote-vla-example" "This could be coordinates (e.g. $$x,y,z$$ in physical space) for some set of robotic joints to move into resulting in some step/action being made. Visualise it like this: think of your brain sending a message to your elbow: 'move to be horizontally up by 5 cm, rotate 10 degrees outwards...' together with similar coordinated messages to other joints in your body, so as to fold a piece of clothing. For more details and a survey of VLA models, see this nice post by <a href='https://x.com/moritz_reuss' title='@moritz_reuss on X.com'>Moritz Reuss</a> on <a href='https://mbreuss.github.io/blog_post_iclr_26_vla.html' title='State of VLA Research at ICLR 2026'>State of VLA Research at ICLR 2026</a>." %} Have a watch of the following video of the <span style='color: #000080'>__$$\pi_{0.5}$$__</span> model from <a href='https://www.pi.website' title='Physical Intelligence'>Physical Intelligence</a> for some idea of what is involved. 

{% youtube "mv-pi05" "https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/Zn8yMaepzVk?si=fFaVXhhiR_LJWglO" "" %}

The latter (WM) is essentially a model that can give a useful prediction of some future outcome(s) given some existing conditions/state. It is an active area of research with contesting visions (possibly eventually complementary) even of what the definition of the term "_world model_" should be; what one such model should be able to do and what forms of predictions (e.g. sequence of robot actions, some group/societal responses etc) it ought to be able to give. This post touches on one form (video models, see [section below on VLAs for robots and autonomous driving](#-3-give-a-concrete-feel-about-these-ideas)); A list of well-written overviews of the different visions can be found in the sidenote here.{% sidenote "sidenote-worldmodel" "See this categorisation of the contesting visions of WMs by <a href='https://x.com/zhuokaiz/status/2032201769053212682?s=20'>@zhuokaiz on X.com</a>, to which I will add: (1) the symbolic-based effort of <a href='https://moonlakeai.com/blog/why-world-models-need-structure-not-just-scale' title='Why World Models Need Structure, Not Just Scale'>Moonlake</a> led by Christoper Manning, and (2) the notions of some kind of _social world models_ <a href='https://openreview.net/forum?id=vC6DGcAdWR' title='Social World Models: Universal Structured Representations for Social Reasoning'>(Zhou et al, 2025)</a>. There is also interest for a <a href='https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.17365' title='Computer-Using World Model'>Computer-Using World Model</a>, which will help contextualise this recently announced <a href='https://www.reuters.com/sustainability/boards-policy-regulation/meta-start-capturing-employee-mouse-movements-keystrokes-ai-training-data-2026-04-21/?mc_cid=eda7c588f4' title='Meta to start capturing employee mouse movements, keystrokes for AI training data'>move by Meta</a>. For a deeper context on WMs, see also see the oft-cited 2018 work by <a href='https://worldmodels.github.io/' title='Recurrent World Models Facilitate Policy Evolution'>Ha (now of SakanaAI) & Schmidhuber</a> and <a href='https://people.idsia.ch/~juergen/world-model-boom.html' title='The Neural World Model Boom'>the latter's recent personal contextualisation</a> of the subject." %}

### 🤷‍♂️ 2. Why should we care about these models?
MLLMs can also have applications beyond the ones above (that of interacting with AI models or for robots/vehicles to think, plan and act). These models (defined as being able to understand and/or generate over two or more modalities e.g. vision-language) look set to touch many aspects of our daily live. 

We have heard of how generative AI has the potential to bring advancements to healthcare, education, public safety, and MLLMs will expand those spaces. 
Less visible are the ways they can also be used to speed up scientific discovery{% sidenote "sidenote-microvqa" "See the <a href='https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content/CVPR2025/papers/Burgess_MicroVQA_A_Multimodal_Reasoning_Benchmark_for_Microscopy-Based_Scientific_Research_CVPR_2025_paper.pdf' title='MicroVQA: A Multimodal Reasoning Benchmark for Microscopy-Based Scientific Research'>MicroVQA benchmark</a> at CVPR 2025 put together by trained biologists for VQA over a highly challenging suite of tasks involving (1) visual understanding of microscope images, then (2) generating hypotheses over the observations from the images, and then (3) experiments proposal. The key insight are results (Table 3) on the benchmark showing that Claude 3.5 Sonnet can reach levels for tasks (1) and (2) that outperform a human baseline (from answers of trained biologists) on the benchmark, and near-comparable levels for task (3). There is also research ongoing to add capabilities such as personalisation and memory, for e.g. <a href='https://personavlm.github.io/' title='PersonaVLM: Long-Term Personalized Multimodal LLMs'>(Nie et al, 2026)</a>, that will give rise to personal productivity applications that are more embedded and "sticky", which will impact us as consumers of these future products. Given the potential wide-ranging reach of such models (and AI in general), the more that we each have an understanding (at the minimum, to make informed personal decisions for individual flourishing), and participate in discussions of what these technologies might look like, including who builds them, why and how they build and run them this way, the better-positioned we are to ask for and construct a meaningfully inclusive future.{% sidenote "sidenote-readings" "<ins>_Sidenote:_</ins> a list of potentially interesting books along this direction can be found [here](https://hankelvin.github.io/articles/26/Reading_List_0426)." %}

### 🧱 3. Give a concrete feel about these ideas?
The Physical Intelligence <span style='color: #000080'>__$$\pi_{0.5}$$__</span> video above would have given a sense of what how language and vision come together in the VLA for robotics use (user instruction understanding, task decomposition, reasoning and real-time video of the robot's environment). Here, I will widen/narrow our field of vision (depends on how you want to see it) and step away from MLLMs for a bit. By looking into video-only generative models, we can get a look into the idea of WMs. Here I will examine uses in robotics and autonomous driving.{% sidenote "sidenote-alpamayo" "There are VLA solutions for these, in autonomous driving, there is Nvidia's <a href='https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.00088' title='Alpamayo-R1: Bridging Reasoning and Action Prediction for Generalizable Autonomous Driving in the Long Tail'>Alpamayo-R1/R1.5</a> models." %} 

Let's start with robotics, with <span style='color: #000080'>__Large Video Planner__</span> proposed by <a href='https://scenerepresentations.org/publications/large-video-planner' title='Large Video Planner Enables Generalizable Robot Control'>(Chen et al, 2025)</a>. Think of this as Chain-of-Thought and task decomposition that we are used to with LLMs, but in a visual way. The following description and graphic explains what the video planner does and how robot controls are obtained from its output.{% sidenote "sidenote-lvp" "The benefit of <a href='https://scenerepresentations.org/publications/large-video-planner' title='Large Video Planner Enables Generalizable Robot Control'>(Chen et al, 2025)</a>'s approach is that there is a lot of human hand action video that can be collected from the web (i.e. YouTube) which can be used to train some world model that can predict hand actions to complete a task. 
<br>
<ins>_Sidenote:_</ins> That said, there is much more to it and many edge cases to resolve for robotics, see this <a href='https://www.bvp.com/atlas/can-world-models-unlock-general-purpose-robotics' title='Can world models unlock general purpose robotics?'>VC piece</a> on robotics world models for more insights."%}

<br>

<em>A robot foundation model maps observations and goals to a sequence of actions. We realize this through a two-stage design: a large <span style='color: #000080'><ins>video planner</ins></span> followed by <span style='color: #800d00'><ins>action extraction</ins></span>.<br><br> 

Consider a robot facing a door it has never encountered before. Its camera perceives the door handle as its owner instructs, “Open this door.” <br><br>
The robot first employs a video foundation model to <span style='color: #000080'><ins>imagine</ins></span> how a rational human would perform the task—generating a video where a hand reaches for the handle, twists it, and pushes the door open. It then applies action extraction algorithms to <span style='color: #800d00'><ins>translate this visual plan into executable control signals</ins></span>, whether for a dexterous five-fingered hand or a parallel gripper.</em> -- (Chen et al, 2025)
</p>
</div>

The <span style='color: #800d00'><ins>action extraction</ins></span> referred above essentially leverages methods from "classical" computer vision such as pose or [optical flow](https://visionbook.mit.edu/optical_flow.html) estimation, and can be rather involved (see §3.3 of their paper for how they go from predicting human hand movements to obtaining robot hand control coordinates) especially when requiring fine-grained control for hand dexterity here.{% sidenote "sidenote-tesseract" "(_Skip this if you don't need the technical details_) In comparison, <a href='https://tesseractworld.github.io/' title='TesserAct: Learning 4D Embodied World Models'>(Zhen et al, 2025)</a> use optical flow to obtain depth and normal information that is then used to generate 4D (where the 4th 'D' refers to time) point cloud. An 'inverse dynamics model' (see §4.4 of their paper), an MLP that takes 4D point cloud at two frames ($$t$$ and $$t+1$$) where $$t$$ would be the current state, and $$t+1$$ is the predicted state generated by the video planner. The inverse dynamics MLP gives the information for the robotic control, the degrees-of-freedom (DoF) coordinates.
<br>
<ins>_Sidenote:_</ins> For a sense of what a 4D point cloud is (as I was curious to), see the visualisation [here](https://d4rt-paper.github.io/), click on the 'Point Cloud Reconstruction' tab. The many dots in the right part of the visualisation is the 'point cloud' which capture position of the surfaces of the scene over 3D. Notice the square box moving too? That is the camera plane which is moving over time, so essentially the representation captures 4D spatio-temporal information. This type of point clouds would be useful for autonomous driving or site surveyance; similar 4D point clouds can be constructed for human-object interaction (HOI) data in robotics (see for e.g. [this](https://hoi4d.github.io/))." %}

Similar ideas extend to self-driving, where the task is arguably trickier (changing light, rapid shifting of scene elements and occlusions etc). Here we take a look at <span style='color: #000080'>__VISTA__</span> approach <a href='https://opendrivelab.com/Vista/' title='Vista: A Generalizable Driving World Model with High Fidelity and Versatile Controllability'>(Gao et al, 2024)</a> as a reference approach. The model is intended to generate the next frame of a driving scene giving the current frame (i.e. what the scene with its elements is expected to look like at some later, usually immediately after, timestep), and the application is slightly different from <span style='color: #000080'>__Large Video Planner__</span> above. It is used to generate videos with different conditioning which are in turn used for evaluating the quality of possible alternatives (what might happen should the car turn left, right, keep driving forwards etc) to inform driving decision-making. Some generated examples for <span style='color: #000080'>__VISTA__</span> can be seen below; if you look closely you can see various generation artifacts in the videos i.e. there is much work to get to realism.

This should give a grounded sense of why there is the heavy interest for high-fidelity video world models.{% sidenote "sidenote-fidelity" "For <span style='color: #000080'>__Large Video Planner__</span> <a href='https://scenerepresentations.org/publications/large-video-planner' title='Large Video Planner Enables Generalizable Robot Control'>(Chen et al, 2025)</a>, the robot controls are extracted with classical CV methods, the better the quality of the generated video (details, consistency etc) the better the controls. ### 4. 🔭 Contextualising and imagining futures

In fact, the generated driving videos are in a lineage of simulation that can be traced back to the last century <a href='https://movementsofmodels.antikythera.org/#hero-6' title='Antikythera: Some movements of models'>(de Monchaux & Catalogtree, 2026)</a>. Take a look at the following "driving" video shot in the 1960s at the Berkeley Environmental Simulation Lab; source: (de Monchaux & Catalogtree, 2026). The marvel is that it was shot on meticulously made scale models in the 1960s, with technology, techniques and personalities that sit on a historical arc in engineering that span the Panama Canal, military radar systems and films such as 2001: A Space Odyssey and Star Wars.{% sidenote "sidenote-movementmodels" "I highly highly recommend reading this wonderfully written and researched piece by <a href='https://movementsofmodels.antikythera.org/#hero' title='Antikythera: Some movements of models'>(de Monchaux & Catalogtree, 2026)</a> which was just published in <a href='https://journal.antikythera.org/' title='Antikythera: Journal for the Philosophy of Planetary Computation'>Antikythera</a>, itself a very fascinating journal. The article traces the arc of engineering's interest (cybernetics, optimal control) in models for simulation, which if you look closer can see clear lines to what is being sought for in WMs. This contextualisation is valuable for perspective-taking and seeding questions for inquiry. I do hope you take some time to read the conclusion, perhaps revisiting a few times as I did, as each time might draw out further insights and connections for you." %}

Here, I would like to suggest that we might be well-served to ask how these technology(ies) can be developed/calibrated to make us all better and not worse off. Many of us cannot directly shift the arc of where we land in the future, but we can make individual informed decisions regarding our collective hopes/wishes/desires/plans that translate into attention, public discourse and quotidian actions that collectively tilt the arc. We could ask for (especially as consumers) where we wish these technologies to be and whose wellbeing they need to serve; it is better to do this sooner rather than later, before it is too late -- in the sprint and hustle to build fast in the face of such intense competition, the path of least resistance is often key to survival; our _silence is acquiescence_, and systems once in place carry weight, energy and momentum of their own.
